Welkom in Nederland!
From 2018 to 2020, I was able to go on an 18-month Erasmus exchange in the Netherlands at one of the most prestigious partner universities of the EBP program. The University of Groningen (founded in 1614) and its Bachelor of Science in International Business taught me all the academic rigour and codes during varied and demanding work. It's a whole different culture (and not just transportation) that I have come to love and understand. This exchange was both rewarding and challenging. Much of the curriculum focused on econometrics, statistics (via SPSS) and cross-cultural management.

Incoterms, forecasts & Kanban
After this exchange in the Netherlands and asking myself what I wanted to do later in my professional life, I thought about my first professional experience in maritime transit of wines and spirits. Coming from a family of road hauliers, I decided to make logistics and supply chain my vocation. So I applied for Kedge's Master of Science in Supply Chain (MSc. This high-level training, recognized in France and Europe, will give me all the elements to join one of the aeronautical, automotive or pharmaceutical groups that I admire since my younger years as a manager or supply chain analyst.

Professional Experiences

First steps in international logistics
During the summer of 2017, I decided to do an optional internship to accumulate experience that would be useful later. The German group JF Hillebrand, the leading forwarding agent in the wine and spirits market, accepted me on its site in Bruges (France) as an export documentation assistant. I, therefore, had to manage the exports of alcoholic beverages from the producer to the port of departure, including customs, administrative and logistical formalities and the booking of containers and transport vessels. This first experience was incredibly rich in learning, particularly from a soft skills point of view.

4-star expatriation
Then I took my things and my car to Belgium, near Brussels and Waterloo, to join the Wyndham hotel group and the Dolce chain as Convention and Conference Service Manager for their 4-star hotel and conference centre. This hotel had the peculiarity of being the Deloitte Europe Middle East and Africa group (Deloitte University). I was, therefore, responsible for all the events of the hotel's biggest customer, from their design, organization, progress to final invoicing and feedback. From January to May 2018, this internship allowed me to meet essential interlocutors worldwide, develop my sense of priorities, and place the customer at the center of my attention to guide my efforts towards the quality of service optimal. It was my first expatriation.

Back to Toulouse
During the summer of 2020, in the midst of the COVID-19 pandemic and not to remain passive in the face of the challenges that presented themselves, I joined the AJK Engineering design office in Toulouse... This office, which usually works with the aerospace sector, has developed a high-start, contactless and secure hydroalcoholic gel terminal for high-traffic public places. I was the project's commercial manager, and I had to put in place the entire commercial development strategy of the product in the south of France. Under the direct responsibility of the commercial director and founder of the office, I deployed everything I had learned in business school and my previous internships to make our solution known to as many people as possible.

All Hands on Deck

In March 2021, it is ST Management's turn to trust me and entrust me with an essential task in my curriculum and their activity: that of buyer and supplier for the high-tonnage vessels they manage.

All Hands on Deck
In March 2021, it is ST Management's turn to trust me and entrust me with an essential task in my curriculum and their activity: that of buyer and supplier for the high-tonnage vessels they manage. Therefore, I was in charge of purchasing all consumables on board the ships (food, spare parts, maintenance, fuel...), whether long-term or emergency purchases. This data-driven position, unprecedented for me, allowed me to make a crucial link between purchasing and the supply chain itself, to understand and apprehend the entire value chain of companies.
